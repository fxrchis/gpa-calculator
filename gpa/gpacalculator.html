How to calculate your GPA

Scroll to the Calculator page or click the "Calculator" title on the header.

Once doing so, input the year you enrolled into highschool, the class type you have for each period, and the current grade.

Click "Add Class", and your inputted information will be displayed on a grid.

Keep adding all the classes that you currently have until you are done.

When finished adding all classes, click the "Calculate GPA" button, and your unweighted and weighted GPA will be displayed.

If you want to clear the classes you currently have, next to the "Calculate GPA" button, clicking the "Clear" button will reset all inputted data including year of enrollment, grades, and classes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my GPA?

On the calculator page, by simply entering your enrollment date and entering the class types including the grade of the class, you can input your information to a table showing the information by pressing "Add Class". By clicking the "Calculate" button, both your weighted and unweighted GPA will appear.

Can I set specific academic goals in the goal tracker, and if so, how?

Of course! You can set any type of goal that you want in the goal tracker. In order to create a goal, head to the Analysis page and input any goal you'd like! Once doing so, clicking the "Set Goal" button will show your goals.

How does the "what if" calculator work, and what scenarios can I explore with it?

The calculator makes a prediction of how many units your GPA will increase or decrease. Based off if your class grade decreases or increases to the what if grade, the only two scenarios are showing if your GPA will increase and decrease.

Can I customize the GPA calculator to factor in different grading systems or weighted courses?

Unfortunately no, we have already set the factors to take into account the grading system. Based off the year you have enrolled into highschool and the class types you take, the added weighted GPA points will vary by 0.5 and 1.0, or 0.4 and 0.8.

How can I use the data provided in the academic performance report?

Our goal is to let you interpret on what you want to complete for the future with the data given. Your GPA and our report show the recommendations on how to handle with the situation your in, using your data, it is best to reflect and understand what YOU can do to achieve your goals!
